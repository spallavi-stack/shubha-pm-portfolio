The short version

What Flexy is

US electricity bills are up 26% in five years, and households on dynamic or time-of-use rates have no real way to track hour-by-hour prices themselves. Flexy is an app a utility hands to its own customers, doing two things well: it shows what electricity costs right now, and it automatically shifts EV charging into the cheapest hours. The MVP is scoped to what's actually buildable, EV charging is the only device Flexy automates, and every decision below is grounded in real Illinois program data and real competitor behavior.

Limitations

What this doesn't prove yet

A polished clickable prototype can look further along than it actually is. It's a useful tool for thinking through a product and communicating it, but on its own it doesn't prove people would actually use or pay for the thing.[1]

01

Mostly scripted, one screen is real

The Price & Cost screen fetches ComEd's actual public hourly pricing data live, or falls back to real captured/averaged ComEd data (including a genuine multi-day Week average and real sampled days across Month and Year) if the live fetch can't reach ComEd from wherever this is opened. The load profile behind the cost math is still a modeled shape, since real usage needs the still-gated Green Button Connect partnership. Every other screen runs on scripted, canned states.

02

No real traffic or usage signal

No A/B test, opt-in pilot, or rollout behind any of these numbers, only research and reasoning.

03

No real users

Personas and interviews are AI-generated in character, without a single real conversation with a real ComEd customer behind them.

04

Home charger control, genuinely unsolved

A real feasibility check found ComEd's pricing API and Smartcar are viable, but no public API surfaced for the home chargers the amperage-cap setting would need. That's a named, open risk now, not an assumption.

What would close the gap

Isolating the single riskiest assumption (would a household trust automated charging enough to rely on it) and running the smallest real test against it, alongside resolving the home-charger integration question flagged in the technical feasibility doc.[2] That's the deliberate next phase.
