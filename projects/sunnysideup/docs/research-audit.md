## Flagged claims

1. **~~Missing coverage: leasehold/tenancy permission requirements.~~ RESOLVED.** `scope.md` explicitly named "leaseholders can't modify the building exterior without freeholder/management-company sign-off" and renter landlord-permission needs as the defining constraint for the no-roof-access segment — the original pass of `grounding-research.md` didn't cover this. A follow-up research pass added a "Leasehold and tenancy consent requirements" subsection. It surfaced a notable pattern worth carrying forward: the specific "renters need zero landlord permission for a removable plug-in kit" claim traces exclusively to sites with a commercial incentive to say so, and is absent from the one independent legal source found (Pinsent Masons) — flagged as an unverified, sales-favorable claim rather than settled law. This is now itself the most important open question in the document (see below).

2. **Label bundling: solid directional claims mixed with disputed specific figures under one [Fact] tag.** Example: "2025 was a record year for MCS-certified installations (reported between 257,397 and 269,000 depending on source)... a 31–37% increase" sits inside a single Fact-labeled sentence, even though the specific count is explicitly disputed across sources. Same pattern in the SEG mechanics claim, which ends "verify directly before publishing" inside a Fact tag. Suggested fix: split disputed specific figures into their own Assumption-labeled sentence rather than nesting inside a Fact-labeled paragraph — as written, a reader skimming by label alone would take the disputed number at full Fact confidence.

3. **Uneven corroboration depth within the "Fact" tier.** G98/G99 (named primary engineering-recommendation documents with specific issue numbers) and VAT treatment (two independent professional bodies, ICAEW + AccountingWEB) rest on meaningfully stronger sourcing than the CMA/Ofgem investigation timelines (multiple secondary legal-analysis summaries describing a primary case page, none directly read) or the SIF/NIA funding figures (largely traceable to the regulator's own stated numbers plus one likely-derivative trade-press mention). All currently sit under the same [Fact] label without distinguishing tier. Suggested fix: not necessarily a relabel, but worth naming that "corroborated via independent primary documents" and "corroborated via multiple secondaries describing one primary source" are both reasonably trustworthy but not equally strong — readers relying on the SIF/NIA figures for a monetization pitch should know that.

4. **Systemic limitation, already disclosed but worth restating here.** No claim in this document was personally verified by directly reading a primary source — every fetch attempt, by the research agents and independently in this session, was blocked. The document's methodology note discloses this prominently, which is exactly right, but it's the single biggest reason nothing here should be treated as fully final regardless of individual labels.

## Overall read

This is an unusually disciplined document for AI-generated research — it consistently distinguishes sourced claims from reasoned inference from flagged assumptions, and it's honest about a real tooling failure rather than papering over it. The strongest sections (G98/G99, VAT, the Which? cold-calling survey) rest on genuinely solid, named sourcing. The weakest section (plug-in solar) correctly flags itself as weakest. The one finding that actually matters before moving forward is #1 — a real completeness gap against what the scope doc called for, not a sourcing-quality issue. Recommend a short, targeted follow-up pass on leasehold/tenancy consent law before drafting personas; the labeling nits (#2, #3) are worth a light pass but aren't blocking.
