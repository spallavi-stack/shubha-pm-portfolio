# SunnySideUp — Discovery Scope

## Project basics
- Problem area: An independent, honest, plain-English solar viability check covering two product surfaces — traditional rooftop solar and plug-in/balcony PV — for UK homeowners, leaseholders, and renters. Used two ways: (a) before talking to an installer/salesperson, to get an informed baseline, or (b) after getting a quote/pitch, as an unbiased second opinion.
- Target users: split by roof access, not tenure —
  - **Roof access** (freehold homeowners): rooftop solar, larger systems, DNO/G99 territory more likely.
  - **No roof access** (renters *and* leaseholders — leaseholders own equity but typically can't modify the building exterior without freeholder/management-company sign-off): plug-in/balcony PV.
- Status: full rework — no usable prior material.
- Known constraint: UK solar/renewable policy is outside personal domain experience → grounding research goes deep (policy + market + technical).
- Climate angle: solar (rooftop and plug-in) sits in climate-mitigation territory with real potential impact on UK household emissions — relevant to both the product's mission framing and the monetization hypothesis below.

## Monetization hypothesis
Leading hypothesis: revenue comes from a source aligned with the "independent" positioning rather than installer referral/lead-gen fees — e.g. a climate/public-interest grant, a utility or DNO innovation-fund partnership, or an institutional partnership in the same spirit as Flexy's B2B2C utility relationship, rather than a per-referral fee that would reward telling more people "yes." This is a hypothesis, not a fact — grounding research should look for real UK mechanisms this could plausibly map to (e.g. DNO innovation funding, climate-tech grants, Ofgem-adjacent programs) rather than inventing one from nothing.

## In scope for this pass

- **Problem statement** — framing that covers both product surfaces, both use-timings, and both roof-access segments; names what's broken today (sales pressure, opacity, complexity, and a market that doesn't clearly serve leaseholders/renters at all).
- **Grounding research — regulatory** — UK solar incentive/policy landscape (Smart Export Guarantee vs. dynamic export tariffs, VAT treatment, permitted development rules for rooftop) *and* UK plug-in/balcony PV's regulatory status (BS 7671, G98, DNO notification rules) *and* DNO friction for larger rooftop systems (G98 vs. G99). Every specific figure/standard number here gets verified during research and tagged Fact/Inference/Assumption; none of it is pre-trusted.
- **Grounding research — market & sales practices** — market size/potential for both segments, existing UK solar quote-comparison competitors, real sourcing on UK solar sales practices (Which?, Citizens Advice, RECC), and a first look at real UK funding mechanisms for the monetization hypothesis above.
- **Grounding research — technical/economic** — payback-period math including **battery storage** (hybrid inverter + battery ROI and time-of-use arbitrage, not just standalone PV), current UK electricity prices, and plug-in PV's specific technical constraints (e.g. inverter output caps).
- **Personas** — at minimum: a roof-access homeowner and a no-roof-access renter/leaseholder. Whether the "second opinion" use-timing gets its own persona is decided once research is in.
- **Competitive landscape** — folded into market grounding research.
- **Limitations section** (new, mirroring Flexy's) — explicit viability boundaries: no structural roof-load assessment, no LiDAR shade mapping, no precise wiring-path cost estimates; plus a stated caveat on the financial-advice-adjacent nature of any payback estimate.
- **Data-sourcing boundary** — real (redacted) installer quotes and consumer-body data (Which?, Citizens Advice, RECC) are in scope; paid proprietary market-intelligence databases are out of scope.

## Riskiest-assumption log
1. Renters/leaseholders will actively seek out an independent check before buying plug-in PV, rather than just trusting a marketplace listing or installer.
2. Homeowners already mid-sales-conversation will pause to find and use a second-opinion tool, rather than just accepting or rejecting the number they were given.
3. A non-conflicted revenue model exists along the climate/public-interest line hypothesized above — plausible given solar's emissions relevance, but the specific mechanism is unverified and needs grounding.
4. Plug-in solar's UK regulatory status is stable enough, near-term, for product guidance about it not to go stale within the life of this case study.

## Out of scope for this pass
- No roadmap, synthetic interviews, clickable prototype, account-connection guide, AI-collaboration-review, user-stories, or technical-feasibility docs yet — all sequenced after product brief + personas, matching Flexy's build-up order.
